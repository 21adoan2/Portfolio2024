my story

Started in CS

Aside from my love for creative outlets, I’ve always been big on impact. That’s why I initially pursued computer science in hopes of becoming a software engineer -- to be able to help people all over the world.

Failure & Reflection

Failing a Python course made me reevaluate; though I wanted to make a big impact, CS felt too distant from the things I loved to do: creativity and human connection.

Discovering UX

My reflection led me to UX and it was everything I wanted in a career: creativity, problem-solving, interacting with people & practicing empathy. But above all, I guess it was mostly a gut feeling; design just makes me happy :)

Passion & Identity

I leaned on my inclination for art & my affinity for empathy, rooted in my minority identities, to self-learn design while involving myself in design-a-thons and campus organizations. Learning felt natural; I found myself losing track of time in the thick of designing. tldr: I loved it.
